This course is the first semester of introductory level physics sequence for engineering majors. This semester focuses on mechanics, the study of motion. Understanding the motions of objects and their interactions is one of the principal goals of physics. The fundamental laws of mechanics, first enumerated by Isaac Newton in the 17th century, can be applied to an enormous range of phenomena on scales as diverse as dust grains and galaxies. \bigskip \noindent The primary objective of this course is to engage you in a process that is central to physics: modeling physical phenomena by applying a small set of fundamental principles. The modeling process encompasses explaining and predicting physical behaviors; making appropriate approximations and simplifications for complicated physical systems; and communicating results through mathematical and numerical expressions, diagrams and visualizations, graphs, and even “plain English.” \section*{Important Dates} \begin{table}[!h] \centering \begin{tabular}{lr} Midterm \#1 %%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%% \newpage \section*{Student Learning Outcomes:}In completing this course, students should be able to: \begin{itemize}[itemsep=0pt, topsep=0pt, partopsep=0pt] \item \textbf{Organize knowledge} \begin{itemize}[itemsep=0pt, topsep=0pt, partopsep=0pt] \item Articulate the ideas from each chapter, lecture, and make connections between different concepts. \item Organize their content knowledge to apply to solve a particular physical problem. \end{itemize} \item \textbf{Visualize the problem}: \begin{itemize}[itemsep=0pt, topsep=0pt, partopsep=0pt] \item Sketch the physical parameters as appropriate for a particular scenario. \end{itemize} \item \textbf{Math/physics connection} \begin{itemize}[itemsep=0pt, topsep=0pt, partopsep=0pt] \item Explain the physical meaning of the mathematical formula. \item Translate a physical description of a problem to a mathematical formula necessary to solve it. \end{itemize} \item \textbf{Communicate their approach} \begin{itemize}[itemsep=0pt, topsep=0pt, partopsep=0pt] \item Justify and explain their thinking and approach to solve a problem in either written or oral form. \end{itemize} \item \textbf{Develop problem-solving techniques and strategies} \begin{itemize}[itemsep=0pt, topsep=0pt, partopsep=0pt] \item Choose and apply the appropriate problem solving technique to a particular problem. \item Identify/categorize problem-solving techniques based upon underlying principles rather than contexts. \item Identify alternate methods of solving a given problem. \item Recognize common mistakes/wrong turns and learn to recover from those mistakes. \end{itemize} \item \textbf{Expect and check a solution} \begin{itemize}[itemsep=0pt, topsep=0pt, partopsep=0pt] \item Articulate their expectations for the solution to a problem as appropriate. \end{itemize} \end{itemize} \section*{Prerequisites/ Co-requisites:} You should be comfortable with college algebra, trigonometry, and plane
